Environmental issues caused by resource extraction and product lifecycle

Resource Extraction Methods:

Method Environmental Impact
  • Deforestation
  • Loss of biodiversity
  • Increased CO₂ levels (fewer trees to absorb carbon)
  • Soil erosion
  • Disruption of water cycles
  • Mining
  • Landscape destruction
  • Water pollution from runoff
  • Release of toxic chemicals
  • Habitat destruction
  • Drilling
  • Oil spills
  • Water contamination
  • Air pollution
  • Greenhouse gas emissions
  • Farming
  • Land use changes
  • Water depletion
  • Pesticide/fertilizer runoff
  • Methane emissions (livestock)

Product Mileage (Lifecycle Transportation):

  • Raw material source → Manufacturing: Energy used to transport raw materials
  • Manufacturing → Distribution: Energy used to move finished products
  • Distribution → User location: Energy used for final delivery
  • User location → Final disposal: Energy used for waste management/recycling

Impact: Each transportation stage contributes to carbon footprint through fuel consumption

Carbon Production in Manufacturing:

  • Energy-intensive processes release CO₂
  • Use of fossil fuels for heat/power generation
  • Chemical reactions in some processes emit greenhouse gases
  • Waste products may release carbon when decomposing

Reduction Methods: Renewable energy, process optimization, carbon capture
