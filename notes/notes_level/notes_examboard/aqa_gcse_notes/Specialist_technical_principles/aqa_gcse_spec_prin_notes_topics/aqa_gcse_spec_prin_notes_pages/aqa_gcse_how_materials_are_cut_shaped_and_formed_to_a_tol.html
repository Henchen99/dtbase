How materials are cut shaped and formed to a tolerance

Tolerance:

  • Tolerance is the allowable limit of variation in a physical dimension (e.g. ±0.5mm).
  • Used to ensure parts fit together correctly during assembly.
  • Materials can be cut, shaped or formed using tools that are measured to strict limits (e.g. CNC machines, jigs, moulds).
  • Important for interchangeable parts and quality control.

Manufacture to Minimum and Maximum Measurements:

  • Products are manufactured within a specified range: minimum size to maximum size.
  • If a part is outside this range, it is considered defective or scrap.
  • This ensures consistency, especially in mass production.

Using Tolerances for Quality Control and Prototyping:

  • Tolerances are checked using tools like calipers, micrometers and gauges.
  • Prototypes should be measured and tested against tolerance specs before final production.
  • Consistent checking ensures all parts fit and function correctly in a final product.
  • Reduces waste, increases efficiency, and ensures high product quality.
