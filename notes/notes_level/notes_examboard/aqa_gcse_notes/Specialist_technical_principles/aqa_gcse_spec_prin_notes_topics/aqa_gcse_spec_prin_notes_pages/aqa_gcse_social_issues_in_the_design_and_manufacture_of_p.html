Social issues in the design and manufacture of products

Safe working conditions

Aspect Details
Common Hazards
  • Sharp tools and machinery
  • Dust, fumes, and noise
  • Tripping hazards from cables or clutter
  • Electric shock risk from damaged equipment
Safety Measures
  • Use of PPE (e.g. goggles, gloves, ear defenders)
  • Training on correct tool and machine use
  • Machine guards and emergency stop buttons
  • Good lighting and ventilation
Workplace Practices
  • Regular equipment checks and maintenance
  • Clear walkways and tidy workstations
  • Proper tool and material storage
  • Signage for hazards and safety zones
Legal Requirements
  • Health and Safety at Work Act (1974)
  • Employers must ensure a safe environment
  • Employees must follow safety rules and report risks

Oceanic and Atmospheric Pollution

Pollution Type Causes and Effects
Oceanic Pollution
  • Agricultural runoff: Pesticides and fertilisers washed into rivers and seas
  • Toxic chemicals: Mercury and lead entering food chains and water supplies
  • Plastic waste: Non-degradable plastics accumulating in ocean gyres
  • Oil spills: From extraction and tanker accidents
  • Oil and sewage: Contaminating ecosystems and marine life
  • Microbeads: Banned in cosmetics but persist in oceans (size <1mm)
Atmospheric Pollution
  • Acid rain: NOx and SO₂ combining to damage lakes and soil
  • Carbon monoxide: Contributing to greenhouse gases
  • Carbon dioxide: Vehicle emissions lowering air quality
  • Particulates: Causing global dimming by restricting sunlight

Health Impacts:

  • Lower air quality affects vulnerable groups (young, elderly, asthma sufferers)
  • Increased healthcare costs and mortality rates
  • At-risk groups advised to stay indoors during poor air quality days
