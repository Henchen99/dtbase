How to shape and form using cutting, abrasion and addition

Papers and Boards

Cutting Card:

  1. Use a sharp craft knife with metal ruler on self-healing mat.
  2. Make multiple light passes (3-4) for clean edges.
  3. Always cut away from your body.

Creasing/Scoring:

  1. Mark fold line with ruler.
  2. Use bone folder or blunt knife to compress fibers along line.

Perforating:

  1. Use perforation wheel or rotary cutter.
  2. Space cuts 2-3mm apart for easy tearing.

Timber-based Materials

Cutting:

  1. Hand saw: Use tenon saw for precision, 45° cutting angle.
  2. Power saw: Circular saw with guide rail for straight cuts.

Drilling:

  1. Use brad-point bit for clean holes.
  2. Clamp workpiece to prevent splintering.

Chiseling:

  1. Sharp chisel (25° bevel) with mallet.
  2. Cut with grain direction to prevent tear-out.
  3. Keep both hands behind cutting edge.

Sanding:

  1. Start coarse (80 grit), finish fine (220 grit).
  2. Sand with grain to avoid scratches.

Planing:

  1. Set plane to thin shavings (0.2mm depth).
  2. Check for square with try-square.

Metal-based Materials

Cutting:

  1. Sheet metal: Tin snips for thin, angle grinder for thick.
  2. Bar stock: Hacksaw or bandsaw.

Drilling:

  1. Use cutting fluid (e.g., WD-40 for aluminium).
  2. Center punch before drilling to prevent bit wandering.

Turning (Lathe):

  1. Secure workpiece in 3-jaw chuck.
  2. Use carbide tool bits at appropriate RPM.

Milling:

  1. Clamp workpiece securely to bed.
  2. Use climb milling for better finish.

Casting:

  1. Melt metal in crucible furnace.
  2. Pour into preheated sand mold.

Brazing:

  1. Clean joints, apply flux.
  2. Heat to 450°C, apply filler rod.

Welding:

  1. MIG: 75% Argon/25% CO₂ gas mix.
  2. Maintain consistent travel speed.

Polymers

Cutting:

  1. Sheet: Acrylic scored with knife and snapped.
  2. 3D prints: Flush cutters for supports.

Drilling:

  1. Low RPM to prevent melting.
  2. Use sharp drill bits with 60° point angle.

Casting:

  1. Mix resin and hardener precisely.
  2. Pour into silicone mold, degas in vacuum chamber.

Deforming (Thermoforming):

  1. Heat sheet until pliable (e.g., PETG at 160°C).
  2. Press into mold with vacuum.

3D Printing:

  1. Level print bed.
  2. Set correct nozzle temp (e.g., PLA 200°C).

Welding:

  1. Use plastic welding gun with matching rod.
  2. Melt both surfaces before joining.

Textile-based Materials

Sewing:

  1. Use appropriate needle (e.g., ballpoint for knits).
  2. Standard stitch length 2.5mm.

Pleating:

  1. Mark fold lines with chalk.
  2. Press with iron before stitching.

Gathering:

  1. Two rows of long basting stitches (4mm length).
  2. Pull threads to gather evenly.

Quilting:

  1. Sandwich batting between fabric layers.
  2. Sew through all layers in pattern.

Piping:

  1. Cut bias strips 2× cord diameter + seam allowance.
  2. Sew with zipper foot close to cord.

Electronic/Mechanical Systems

Cutting:

  1. PCB: Use scoring tool or guillotine cutter.
  2. Enclosures: Nibblers for sheet metal.

Drilling:

  1. Use HSS drill bits for PCBs.
  2. Peck drilling (in/out) to clear chips.

Soldering:

  1. Clean tip, apply fresh solder.
  2. Heat joint for 1-2 sec before applying solder.
