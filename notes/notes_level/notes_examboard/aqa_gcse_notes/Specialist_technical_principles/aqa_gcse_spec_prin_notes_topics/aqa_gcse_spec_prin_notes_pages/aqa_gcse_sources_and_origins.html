Sources and origins

Paper and Board

How cellulose fibers are derived and converted into paper:

  • Wood or grasses are broken down mechanically or chemically
  • Mechanical pulping: grinding wood to separate fibers (lower quality)
  • Chemical pulping (kraft process): using chemicals to dissolve lignin
  • Fibers are washed, bleached, and mixed with water to create pulp
  • Pulp is spread on screens, pressed, and dried to form paper
  • Finishing processes may include coating or calendaring

Timber Based Materials

  • Seasoning: Reducing moisture content through air drying or kiln drying
  • Conversion: Sawing logs into boards (plain sawing, quarter sawing)
  • Manufactured timbers:
    • Plywood: Thin wood veneers glued in alternating directions
    • MDF: Wood fibers bonded with resin under heat/pressure
    • Chipboard: Wood chips compressed with adhesive
    • Laminated timber: Layers of wood glued together

Metal Based Materials

Extraction and refining processes:

  • Mining: Ores extracted via open-pit or underground mining
  • Concentration: Removing gangue through processes like flotation
  • Extraction methods:
    • Pyrometallurgy (high temperature smelting)
    • Hydrometallurgy (chemical leaching)
    • Electrometallurgy (electrolysis)
  • Refining: Further purification (electrolytic refining, zone refining)
  • Alloying: Mixing metals to enhance properties

Polymers

  • Crude oil refining: Separation via fractional distillation
  • Fractional distillation: Heating crude oil to separate components by boiling points
  • Cracking: Breaking large hydrocarbon molecules into smaller ones (thermal or catalytic)
  • Polymerization:
    • Addition polymerization (chain growth)
    • Condensation polymerization (step growth with byproducts)

Textile Based Materials

  • Sources:
    • Animal: Wool, silk
    • Vegetable: Cotton, linen
    • Chemical: Polyester, nylon, acrylic
  • Processing:
    • Natural fibers: Cleaning, carding, combing
    • Synthetic fibers: Extrusion through spinnerets
  • Spinning: Drawing out and twisting fibers into yarn
  • Finishing: Dyeing, printing, or applying treatments
