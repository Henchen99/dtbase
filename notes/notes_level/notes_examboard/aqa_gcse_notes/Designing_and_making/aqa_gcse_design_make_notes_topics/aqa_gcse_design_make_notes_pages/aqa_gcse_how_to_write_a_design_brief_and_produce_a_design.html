How to write a design brief and produce a design and manufacturing specifcation

What needs to be covered in a design brief?

1. Project Definition

  • Scope & Purpose
    • Concise problem statement (e.g., "Design ergonomic garden tools for arthritis patients")
    • Clear boundaries of what's included/excluded
  • Background Rationale
    • Market gap analysis
    • User pain points identified through research

2. Core Objectives

  • Primary Goals
    • Functional: "Reduce required grip strength by 40%"
    • User experience: "Achieve 90% comfort rating in trials"
  • Success Metrics
    • Quantifiable targets with measurement methods
    • Example: "15% faster task completion in timed tests"

3. Target Users

  • Detailed Personas
    • Demographics: Age 65+, limited hand mobility
    • Behavioural patterns: Weekly gardening habits
  • Inclusive Considerations
    • Accessibility requirements
    • Cultural/regional usage factors

4. Budget & Resources

  • Financial Plan
    • Total budget: £XX,XXX breakdown
    • Prototyping: 40% | Testing: 25% | Production: 35%
  • Resource Allocation
    • Materials sourcing strategy
    • Specialist consultancy requirements

5. Timeline & Deliverables

  • Phased Schedule
    • Research phase: 4 weeks (incl. user interviews)
    • Prototype development: 6 weeks (3 iterations)
    • User testing: 3 weeks (50 participants)
  • Tangible Outputs
    • Phase 1: User research report + CAD concepts
    • Phase 2: 3D printed prototypes + testing data

How to write a design and manufacturing specification

1. Technical Product Specification

  • Dimensional Requirements
    • Critical measurements with tolerances (e.g., "Grip diameter: 35mm ±0.5mm")
    • Weight restrictions and balance points
  • Material Specifications
    • Precise material grades (e.g., "Polypropylene, 30% glass-filled")
    • Surface finish requirements (texture, roughness values)

2. Production Requirements

  • Manufacturing Processes
    • Specific methods (e.g., "Injection moulding - 2 cavity tool")
    • Cycle time targets and output rates
  • Assembly Instructions
    • Step-by-step assembly sequence
    • Fastening torque values where applicable

3. Quality Control Standards

  • Testing Protocols
    • Performance tests (e.g., "5000 cycle durability test")
    • Safety certifications required (BS/EN standards)
  • Inspection Criteria
    • Visual inspection standards
    • Sampling frequency for dimensional checks

4. Compliance Documentation

  • Regulatory Requirements
    • UKCA/CE marking specifications
    • Product safety directives
  • Sustainability Targets
    • Recyclability percentages
    • Carbon footprint reduction goals

Key Connections Between Brief and Specification

  • User requirements from brief directly inform technical specifications
  • Budget constraints determine material and process selections
  • Timeline impacts tooling complexity and production planning
