Why and How Designers Modify Briefs

1. Reasons for Brief Alterations

  • New Research Findings
    • Emerging user needs not initially considered
    • Technical constraints discovered during prototyping
  • Market Changes
    • New competitor products entering the market
    • Regulatory updates affecting product requirements
  • Practical Constraints
    • Budget adjustments requiring scope changes
    • Material availability issues

2. Process for Brief Modification

  • Impact Assessment
    • Evaluate how changes affect timelines/budgets
    • Consult with all stakeholders (clients, engineers etc.)
  • Version Control
    • Clear documentation of all brief iterations
    • Highlighted changes with rationale
  • Approval Process
    • Formal sign-off required for major changes
    • Updated success metrics where needed

3. Examples of Common Modifications

  • Adjusting target user demographics after research
  • Adding/removing features based on prototyping results
  • Changing material specifications for cost/performance
