Use primary and secondary data to understand client and/or user needs

  • Market Research & Interviews:
    • Conducting large-scale surveys to identify consumer preferences (e.g., smartphone ergonomics studies)
    • Structured interviews with target users to uncover usability issues (e.g., medical device interface testing)
    • Data analysis to spot trends in product complaints/returns

  • Human Factors & Ergonomics:
    • Biomechanical modeling of repetitive motions (e.g., assembly line workstation design)
    • Eye-tracking studies for optimal control panel layouts (e.g., automotive dashboards)
    • Pressure mapping for seating designs (e.g., office chairs, airline seats)

  • Focus Groups:
    • Moderated sessions with 6-10 representative users (e.g., testing kitchen appliance prototypes)
    • A/B testing different product variants (e.g., comparing grip designs for power tools)
    • Recording emotional responses through facial coding software

  • Product Analysis:
    • Disassembling competitor products for material/construction analysis
    • Usability scoring systems (e.g., System Usability Scale for digital interfaces)
    • Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) for safety-critical products

  • Anthropometric Data:
    • National population datasets (e.g., UK Adult Data Survey, US NHANES)
    • 3D body scanning for custom fit products (e.g., ergonomic bicycle frames)
    • Dynamic measurements for motion ranges (e.g., automotive pedal reach studies)

  • Percentile Applications:
    • 5th percentile female to 95th percentile male range for public products
    • Extreme percentile accommodation (e.g., adjustable car seat rails for tall/short drivers)
    • Specialized datasets (e.g., child anthropometrics for educational furniture)

Implementation Framework:

  1. Phase 1: Qualitative Research (Interviews/Focus Groups)
  2. Phase 2: Quantitative Analysis (Anthropometric/Ergonomic Testing)
  3. Phase 3: Iterative Prototyping (3D Modeling/Usability Trials)
  4. Phase 4: Validation Testing (EMG/SEMG for muscle strain evaluation)
Note: All methods should comply with ISO 9241 (Ergonomics of Human-System Interaction) and ISO 15535 (Anthropometric Database Requirements)
