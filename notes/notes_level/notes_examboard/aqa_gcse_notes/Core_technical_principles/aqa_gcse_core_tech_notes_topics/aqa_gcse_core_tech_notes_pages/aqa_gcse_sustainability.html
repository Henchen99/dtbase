Sustainability

Finite Resources:

  • Definition: Non-renewable materials with limited quantities (takes millions of years to form)
  • Examples: Fossil fuels, rare earth metals (neodymium for magnets), phosphate for fertilizers
  • Impact: 90% of biodiversity loss linked to resource extraction (UNEP 2019)
  • Solutions: Urban mining (recovering metals from e-waste), bio-based alternatives

Non-Finite Resources:

  • Definition: Renewable materials if managed responsibly
  • Examples: Bamboo (grows 1m/day), solar energy (173,000 TW hits Earth continuously)
  • Challenges: Overharvesting can make them de facto finite (e.g., deforestation)
  • Innovations: Mycelium packaging, algae-based bioplastics

Waste Disposal Impacts:

  • Landfills: 2 billion tonnes/year global waste (World Bank), methane emissions
  • Ocean Plastic: 5.25 trillion particles circulating (Great Pacific Garbage Patch)
  • E-Waste: 57 million tonnes/year (2021), only 17% formally recycled
  • Solutions: Circular economy models, extended producer responsibility (EPR) laws

Case Studies:

  • Positive: Sweden's 99% waste recycling rate via energy recovery
  • Negative: Fast fashion producing 92 million tonnes/year of textile waste
