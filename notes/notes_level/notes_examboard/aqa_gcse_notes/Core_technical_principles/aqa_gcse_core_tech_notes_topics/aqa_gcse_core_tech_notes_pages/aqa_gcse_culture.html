Culture

  • Cultural influences shape product design, materials and manufacturing processes
  • Technological advancements drive changes in fashion and consumer trends
  • Designers must consider diverse cultural values and religious requirements

Technology & Fashion:

Emergent Technologies Cultural Impacts
  • 3D printed clothing
  • Smart fabrics with embedded sensors
  • Augmented reality fashion apps
  • Fast fashion vs traditional craftsmanship
  • Globalization of design styles
  • Cultural appropriation concerns

Inclusive Design Principles:

  • Respect religious requirements (e.g., modest clothing designs)
  • Accommodate cultural symbols and color meanings
  • Consider regional climate adaptations
  • Address varying economic circumstances

Examples: Hijab-friendly sports gear, culturally appropriate medical devices, localized packaging designs

Nike Air Bakin' "Flame" Controversy (1997):

Issue Outcome
  • Rear logo design resembled Arabic script for God
  • Muslim community protested the sacred symbol being placed on footwear (considered disrespectful)
  • Similar to 1996 Nike Air Shake ND dispute with Hebrew lettering
  • Nike recalled 38,000 pairs and destroyed remaining stock
  • Established religious review process for future designs
  • Case study in cultural sensitivity in product design

Key Learning Points:

  • Global brands must vet designs across all cultural contexts
  • Religious symbols require special handling in commercial products
  • Post-crisis response impacts brand reputation long-term

Modern Applications: Halal sportswear lines, culturally-vetted footwear designs
