Nuclear Power

How Power is Generated Advantages Disadvantages
  • Nuclear fission of uranium/plutonium atoms releases heat
  • Heat produces steam to drive turbines
  • Control rods regulate the chain reaction
  • Zero CO2 emissions during operation
  • Extremely high energy density (1kg uranium = 3,000 tons coal)
  • Continuous power (90%+ capacity factor)
  • Radioactive waste requires secure long-term storage
  • High initial construction costs
  • Public fear of accidents (Chernobyl, Fukushima)
