Industry

Wind

How Power is Generated Advantages Disadvantages
  • Wind turns turbine blades connected to a generator
  • Can be onshore or offshore installations
  • Zero emissions during operation
  • Low operating costs after installation
  • Land can often still be used for agriculture
  • Intermittent (dependent on wind availability)
  • Visual impact and potential noise pollution
  • Can harm birds and bats

Solar

How Power is Generated Advantages Disadvantages
  • Photovoltaic cells convert sunlight directly to electricity
  • Solar thermal systems use mirrors to concentrate heat
  • Abundant and renewable energy source
  • Can be installed at point of use (rooftops)
  • Silent operation with no moving parts
  • Intermittent (no power at night)
  • Requires large areas for utility-scale generation
  • Manufacturing process has environmental impact

Tidal

How Power is Generated Advantages Disadvantages
  • Turbines capture energy from tidal flows
  • Can use tidal barrages or tidal stream generators
  • Highly predictable energy source
  • High energy density of water
  • Long operational lifespan
  • Limited to coastal locations with strong tides
  • High capital costs
  • Potential impact on marine ecosystems

Hydro-electric

How Power is Generated Advantages Disadvantages
  • Flowing water turns turbines in dams or rivers
  • Pumped storage can store energy
  • Reliable and dispatchable power
  • Long lifespan (50-100 years)
  • Can provide flood control and irrigation
  • Large dams disrupt ecosystems
  • Displaces communities
  • Methane emissions from reservoirs

Biomass

How Power is Generated Advantages Disadvantages
  • Organic matter burned directly or converted to biogas
  • Can use wood, crops, or waste materials
  • Can use waste products as fuel
  • Carbon neutral when sustainably managed
  • Provides baseload power
  • Still produces air pollution when burned
  • Can compete with food production for land
  • Lower energy density than fossil fuels
