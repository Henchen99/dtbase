Socio economic influences

Historical and contemporary factors shaping product design and manufacturing approaches.

Post WW1: Bauhaus & Mass Production

  • Revolutionary design school promoting functionalism and industrial production
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Made design accessible to masses
    • Reduced production costs
    • Standardised quality
    • Loss of traditional craftsmanship
    • Limited aesthetic variety

    Examples: Marcel Breuer's tubular steel chairs, modular furniture systems

    WW2: Rationing & Utility Products

  • Wartime constraints driving minimalist, functional design
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Efficient material use
    • Standardised sizing
    • Durability focus
    • Limited consumer choice
    • Aesthetic limitations

    Examples: CC41 utility furniture, simplified clothing designs

    Contemporary Influences

    Factor Impact on Design Advantages Disadvantages
    Fast Fashion Rapid production cycles Affordable trends Environmental impact
    Mass Furniture Flat-pack solutions Space efficiency Disposable culture
    Decorative Design Aesthetic-driven products Personal expression Functionality compromise

    Examples: IKEA furniture, seasonal home decor trends
