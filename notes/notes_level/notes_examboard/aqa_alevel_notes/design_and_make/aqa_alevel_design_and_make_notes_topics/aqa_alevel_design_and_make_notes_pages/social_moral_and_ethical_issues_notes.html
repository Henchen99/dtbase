Social, moral and ethical issues

Designers' responsibilities in creating products that benefit society while minimising harm.

Sustainable Materials

  • Using renewable/recyclable resources in production
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Reduces environmental impact
    • Meets EU sustainability regulations
    • Appeals to eco-conscious consumers
    • Higher material costs
    • Limited availability
    • Performance trade-offs

    Examples: Bamboo textiles, recycled ocean plastics

    Ethical Production Methods

  • Ensuring fair labour practices and safe working conditions
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Improves worker welfare
    • Enhances brand reputation
    • Reduces legal risks
    • Increased production costs
    • Complex supply chain monitoring
    • Longer lead times

    Examples: Fairtrade-certified factories, living wage initiatives

    Culturally Acceptable Design

  • Creating non-offensive products for diverse groups
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Wider market appeal
    • Promotes social cohesion
    • Reduces product recalls
    • Requires extensive research
    • May limit creative expression
    • Cultural sensitivity training costs

    Examples: Modest fashion lines, culturally neutral packaging

    Inclusive Product Development

  • Designing for accessibility and diverse needs
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Expands consumer base
    • Complies with equality laws
    • Improves social equity
    • Increased R&D costs
    • May require multiple product versions
    • Complex user testing

    Examples: OXO Good Grips utensils, Microsoft Adaptive Controller

    Addressing Social Problems

  • Designing products to tackle societal challenges
    • Issue Design Response Impact
    Poverty Low-cost water filters Reduces disease in developing nations
    Health Portable dialysis machines Improves patient mobility
    Housing Flat-pack emergency shelters Quick disaster response

    Fairtrade Influence

  • Ethical sourcing's effect on design practices
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Ensures farmer fair pay
    • Traceable supply chains
    • Premium product positioning
    • Certification costs
    • Limited producer participation
    • Higher retail prices

    Examples: Divine Chocolate, People Tree clothing

    Sustainability: The Six Rs

  • Environmental design principles
    • Principle Design Application Example
    Rethink Questioning product necessity Digital instead of physical
    Refuse Rejecting non-essential features Minimal packaging
    Reduce Material efficiency Lightweighting
    Reuse Multi-life products Refillable containers
    Repair Modular design Fairphone
    Recycle Material recovery Monomer recycling

    Ethical Imperative: Designers must balance commercial success with social responsibility, considering a product's full lifecycle impact.
