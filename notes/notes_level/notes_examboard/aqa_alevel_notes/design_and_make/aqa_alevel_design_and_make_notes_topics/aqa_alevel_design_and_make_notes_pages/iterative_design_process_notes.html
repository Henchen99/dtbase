Iterative design process

Good design addresses multiple issues, including understanding user needs, research, data application, and iterative development.

Designing to Meet Needs, Wants, or Values

  • Focuses on aligning design solutions with user requirements, preferences, and ethical considerations.
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Enhances user satisfaction
    • Promotes inclusivity
    • Aligns with ethical standards
    • Time-consuming research
    • Potential conflicting priorities

    Examples: Customizable products, accessibility features

    Investigations Using Primary/Secondary Data

  • Methods to gather insights informing design decisions:

    • Method Description Advantages Disadvantages
    Market Research Analyzing competitors and consumer trends Identifies market gaps Costly and time-intensive
    Interviews Direct user feedback collection Detailed qualitative data Small sample size
    Human Factors Studying ergonomics and usability Improves safety/comfort Requires expert input
    Focus Groups Group discussions for feedback Diverse perspectives Groupthink risks
    Product Analysis Evaluating existing products Identifies strengths/weaknesses May limit innovation

    Anthropometric Data and Percentiles

  • Applying body measurements to ensure ergonomic fit for target users.

    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Accommodates diverse body sizes
    • Reduces injury risks
    • Data may lack inclusivity
    • Requires regular updates

    Examples: Adjustable office chairs, vehicle cockpit designs

    Ergonomic Data

  • Optimising designs for human comfort, efficiency, and safety.

    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Enhances usability
    • Reduces physical strain
    • May increase production costs
    • Requires iterative testing

    Examples: Ergonomic keyboards, tool handle grips

    Development of Design Proposal

  • Creating a detailed plan outlining specifications, materials, and user benefits.

    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Provides clear direction
    • Facilitates stakeholder communication
    • Time-consuming revisions
    • May limit flexibility

    Examples: CAD models, technical drawings

    Prototype Planning and Manufacture

  • Building a physical/digital model to test functionality and user interaction.

    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Identifies production issues
    • Validates design feasibility
    • High material/time costs
    • May require multiple iterations

    Examples: 3D-printed models, mock-ups

    Prototype Evaluation

  • Testing and refining prototypes based on user feedback and performance data.

    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Improves final product quality
    • Reduces long-term costs
    • Prolongs development timeline
    • Requires user involvement

    Examples: User trials, stress-testing reports
