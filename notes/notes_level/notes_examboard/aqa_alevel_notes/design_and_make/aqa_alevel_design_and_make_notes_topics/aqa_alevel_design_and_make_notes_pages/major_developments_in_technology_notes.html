Major developments in technology

Micro Electronics

  • Miniaturisation of electronic components enabling smart products
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Enables IoT integration
    • Reduces product size
    • Improves energy efficiency
    • Complex manufacturing
    • E-waste challenges
    • Higher R&D costs

    Examples: Smart watches, RFID tags, wearable tech

    New Materials

  • Innovative substances with enhanced properties
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Improved strength/weight ratios
    • Enhanced sustainability
    • Novel functionalities
    • High production costs
    • Recycling challenges
    • Limited availability

    Examples: Graphene, self-healing polymers, aerogels

    New Manufacturing Methods

  • Advanced production techniques transforming industry
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Mass customisation
    • Reduced waste
    • Faster production
    • High initial investment
    • Technical expertise required
    • Energy intensive

    Examples: 3D printing, robotic assembly, laser cutting

    CAD/CAM Advancements

  • Computer-aided design/manufacturing integration
    • Advantages Disadvantages
    • Rapid prototyping
    • Precision engineering
    • Virtual testing
    • Software learning curve
    • High hardware requirements
    • Reduced manual skills

    Examples: Parametric design, CNC machining, FEA analysis

    Conclusion: These technological advancements enable more complex, sustainable, and user-centric designs while requiring multidisciplinary knowledge integration.
