Designers and their work

Designers embody the philosophies of their movements through innovation, materials, and user-centric approaches

Philippe Starck (Postmodernism):

  • Principles: Democratic design, humour, and accessibility for mass markets.
  • Key Works: Juicy Salif citrus squeezer (Alessi), Ghost Chair (Kartell).
  • Design Movement: Postmodernism – rejects minimalism, embraces playful absurdity.
  • Impact: Made high design accessible through everyday objects.


    • James Dyson (Modernism):

  • Principles: Problem-solving through iteration, engineering-led innovation.
  • Key Works: Dual Cyclone vacuum cleaner, Airblade hand dryer.
  • Design Movement: Modernism – prioritises function and technological advancement.
  • Impact: Revolutionised household appliances with cyclonic separation technology.


    • Margaret Calvert (Modernism):

  • Principles: Clarity, universal readability, and user safety.
  • Key Works: UK road signage system, Rail Alphabet typeface.
  • Design Movement: Modernism – functional communication for public spaces.
  • Impact: Standardised visual language for transportation (globally influential).


    • Dieter Rams (Modernism/Bauhaus):

  • Principles: "Less but better", 10 Principles of Good Design (e.g., longevity, honesty).
  • Key Works: Braun SK4 record player, T3 pocket radio.
  • Design Movement: Bauhaus/Modernism – minimalist functionality, user-centricity.
  • Impact: Inspired Apple’s design ethos; benchmark for industrial design.


    • Charles and Ray Eames (Modernism):

  • Principles: Experimentation with materials (plywood, fibreglass), ergonomic comfort.
  • Key Works: Eames Lounge Chair, Molded Plastic Chair.
  • Design Movement: Modernism – mass production of high-quality furniture.
  • Impact: Democratised modernist design for post-war middle-class homes.


    • Marianne Brandt (Bauhaus):

  • Principles: Geometric purity, merging art with industrial production.
  • Key Works: MT49 teapot, Kandem bedside lamp.
  • Design Movement: Bauhaus – "form follows function" in household objects.
  • Impact: Pioneered gender inclusivity in industrial design (rare female Bauhaus designer).
