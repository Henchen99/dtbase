Physical characteristics

Electrical conductivity:

The Property that lets electrical current flow through a material


Thermal conductivity:

The Property that lets thermal energy flow through a material


Biodegradability:

A material's ability to break down over time as a result of biological activity (being broken down by microorganisms)


Chemical resistance:

A material's ability to withstand chemical attack without being broken down



Corrosion resistance:

A material's ability to withstand rusting from water without being broken down


Mechanical (Working) characteristics

Strength (Tensile, Compressive, Torsional, Shear, Bending):

The ability allowing a material to resist forces from all directions (compressive, tensile, bending, shear, torsional).


Elasticity:

The ability allowing an object or material to reassume its normal shape after being stretched or compressed.


Placticity:

A material's ability to be permanently deformed and retain its deformed shape


Malleability:

A materia's ability to be deformed by compression without being torn / cracked.


Ductility:

The ability of a material to be drawn/stretched out thin (usually into wires)


Hardness:

A material's ability to withstand indentation / abrasion / scratching.


Toughness:

A material's ability to absorb impact force without fracture.


Durability:

A material's ability to withstand wear, pressure, and damage.


Product Design Considerations

  • Good product design balances function, aesthetics, cost, and sustainability.
  • Each factor affects usability, manufacturing efficiency, and environmental impact.

Aspect Description
Product Function
  • Defines how well a product performs its intended task.
  • Must be ergonomic and user-friendly.
  • Should be durable and reliable to prevent frequent repairs.
  • Consider safety features to minimise risks.
  • Functionality can be enhanced through modularity (e.g., interchangeable parts).
Aesthetics
  • Focuses on how the product looks and feels.
  • Colour, texture, and form impact customer perception.
  • Trends and cultural influences affect aesthetic preferences.
  • Design should balance form and function.
  • Materials and finishes contribute to perceived quality.
Cost
  • Includes materials, labour, manufacturing, and distribution costs.
  • Cost can be reduced by using standardised parts and efficient manufacturing.
  • Minimising waste in production helps reduce expenses.
  • Target market (luxury vs. budget-friendly) affects cost decisions.
  • Balance cost and quality to maintain durability and affordability.
Manufacture & Disposal
  • Design for efficient manufacturing with minimal steps.
  • Material selection impacts processing and sustainability.
  • Use recyclable materials to promote eco-friendly disposal.
  • Parts should be easy to separate for proper recycling.
  • Reduce use of toxic or harmful substances.
  • Consider end-of-life design (repair, repurpose, or upgrade instead of disposal).
