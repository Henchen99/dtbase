Manufacture, Repair, Maintenance & Disposal

Manufacturing processes:

  • Manufacturing efficiency is crucial for reducing costs and material waste.
  • Modifications to product designs can simplify manufacturing and increase production speed.
  • Fewer processes lead to lower energy consumption and less waste.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Reducing the number of processes manufacturing time and cost.
  • Minimises material waste.
  • Improves product consistency.
  • Requires careful planning and testing.
  • May limit design flexibility.

Material Choice & Disposal:

  • The choice of materials affects the product’s durability, recyclability, and impact on the environment.
  • Labelling materials correctly helps with efficient recycling.
  • Designing products for easy disassembly improves sustainability and reduces landfill waste.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Labels help with material separation for recycling.
  • Disassembly-friendly designs aid end-of-life processing.
  • Use of recyclable materials reduces environmental impact.
  • Recycling infrastructure varies by region.
  • Some eco-friendly materials have higher initial costs.

Sustainability - The Six Rs:

  • The six Rs help reduce environmental impact by guiding material use and disposal.
  • Each principle aims to extend product life, reduce waste, and lower energy consumption.

Principle Description
Reduce Lower material usage, reduce waste and energy consumption.
Reuse Design for reusability of components and parts.
Rethink Choose sustainable materials and eco-friendly alternatives.
Recycle Ensure materials/components can be reprocessed for future use.
Repair Design products to be fixable rather than disposable, extending their lifespan.
Refuse Avoid using non-recyclable or environmentally harmful materials.

Maintenance:

  • Regular maintenance extends product life and reduces the need for replacements.
  • Using standardised parts allows for easy repairs and servicing.
  • Some products can be upgraded with software updates instead of being replaced.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Extends product lifespan.
  • Reduces electronic and material waste.
  • Software updates can improve functionality.
  • Requires standardised parts for compatibility.
  • Some products are designed to be non-repairable.
