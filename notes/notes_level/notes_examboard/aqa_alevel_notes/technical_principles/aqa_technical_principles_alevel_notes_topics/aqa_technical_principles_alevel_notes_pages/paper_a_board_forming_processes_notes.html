Paper and board forming processes

Die Cutting:

How it works:

  • Die cutting uses a sharp, custom-shaped die to cut through materials like paper, cardboard, or plastic.
  • The die is pressed onto the material using a machine, cutting it into a specific shape.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Accurate and precise cuts
  • Can cut through multiple layers at once
  • Ideal for high-volume production
  • Requires custom-made dies, which can be expensive
  • Not suitable for intricate designs or small runs

Uses: Packaging, labels, paper products, and custom cutouts

Laser Cutting:

How it works:

  • A focused laser beam melts, burns, or vaporizes the material to cut through it.
  • Laser cutting is typically used for metals, plastics, wood, boards, and other materials.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Very precise and clean cuts
  • Can cut a wide range of materials
  • Minimal material wastage
  • Can be expensive for small-scale production
  • Heat-affected zone can deform certain materials

Uses: Metal fabrication, sign making, custom parts

Creasing:

How it works:

  • Creasing involves making an indentation or fold line in a material without cutting through it.
  • This is usually done with a die or press to create a clean, straight line for folding.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Helps create sharp, accurate folds
  • Prevents cracking of materials during folding
  • Improves the overall appearance of folded products
  • Not suitable for all materials
  • Requires precise die adjustment

Uses: Packaging, greeting cards, brochures

Bending:

How it works:

  • Bending involves applying force to a material to change its shape without breaking it.
  • Typically done using machines like press brakes or rollers to bend materials such as metal, plastic, or paper.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Can create complex shapes with minimal material wastage
  • Cost-effective for mass production
  • Requires less energy compared to other methods
  • Can cause material deformation if not done correctly
  • Not suitable for very thick materials

Uses: Metal parts, automotive, structural components
