Efficient use of materials

Relationship Between Material Cost, Form, and Manufacturing Processes:

  • Students must understand how material cost affects the form of a product and the choice of manufacturing processes.
  • Manufacturing processes should align with the material's characteristics to ensure cost-efficiency and optimal design.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Optimizes material usage for cost-effectiveness
  • Enhances manufacturing efficiency through appropriate process selection
  • Can increase upfront design costs due to material and process selection
  • Requires a deep understanding of materials and manufacturing processes


Development of Designs Using Materials Economically and with Regard to Their Characteristics:

  • Designers should use materials economically, keeping in mind their specific characteristics to ensure efficient manufacturing.
  • Understanding material properties allows designers to avoid over-engineering or underutilizing materials.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Reduces material wastage and lowers costs
  • Increases the longevity and functionality of the product
  • Requires careful analysis of material properties
  • May limit design flexibility in some cases


Use of Manufacturing Processes to Increase Accuracy and Reduce Waste:

  • Manufacturing processes should be selected to increase the accuracy of product dimensions while minimizing waste.
  • Techniques such as precision tooling or additive manufacturing can help achieve these goals.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Increases product quality and precision
  • Reduces material waste, leading to cost savings
  • Advanced manufacturing processes can require significant investment
  • May increase production time in some cases


Bulk Production vs One-Off Production:

  • Bulk production allows for economies of scale, reducing the cost per unit when producing large quantities
  • One-off production, however, may be more cost-effective for unique or low-volume items
  • Bulk buying of materials reduces the price per unit of the material
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Bulk production significantly reduces the cost per unit
  • Ideal for mass-market products
  • Bulk production can lead to excess inventory if demand is underestimated
  • One-off production may offer more flexibility, but higher cost per unit


Just-in-time (JIT):

Advantages
  • Parts are ordered when needed/automatically
  • Less money tied up in stock
  • Less storage space needed, cost of warehouse spacing reduced
  • High returns for company
  • Reduces the movement of materials, environmental factor
  • Lean manufacturing reduces prodctuon times
  • Can react quickly to market trends
  • Higher levels of quality assurance
  • Increased job satisfaction
  • Reduces labour costs
