Classification of Materials

This page explains the unique properties and examples of different material classifications.

Metals (Ferrous, Non-Ferrous, Alloys):

  • Unique Property: Ferrous metals contain iron, while non-ferrous metals do not.
  • Example: Steel (ferrous), Aluminum (non-ferrous), Bronze (alloy)

Woods (Hardwoods, Softwoods, Manufactured Boards):

  • Unique Property: Hardwoods come from broadleaf trees, while softwoods come from conifers. Manufactured boards are engineered wood products.
  • Example: Oak (hardwood), Pine (softwood), MDF (manufactured board)

Polymers (Thermoplastics, Thermoset Polymers, Elastomers):

  • Unique Property: Thermoplastics can be reheated and reshaped, thermosets form permanent shapes, and elastomers are flexible and stretchable.
  • Example: Polyethylene (thermoplastic), Urea Formaldehyde (thermoset), Rubber (elastomer)

Papers and Boards:

  • Unique Property: Made from cellulose fibers, often used for packaging and writing materials.
  • Example: Cardboard, Tissue paper

Composites:

  • Unique Property: Materials made from two or more different substances to enhance properties.
  • Example: Carbon fibre reinforced plastic (CFRP), Concrete

Smart Materials:

  • Unique Property: Change their properties in response to external stimuli (e.g., temperature, light).
  • Example: Shape-memory alloys, Photovoltaic cells

Modern Materials:

  • Unique Property: Materials developed for advanced technological applications with enhanced performance.
  • Example: Graphene, Carbon nanotubes
