Performance characteristics of polymer based sheet and film

Foam Board:

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Lightweight
  • Easy to cut and shape
  • Can be scored and folded
  • Good rigidity
  • Not very durable
  • Can be easily dented or crushed

Uses: Model making, Presentation boards, Mounting artwork



Fluted Polypropylene:

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Waterproof
  • Lightweight and strong
  • Easy to print on
  • Good impact resistance
  • Can be difficult to fold cleanly
  • Not biodegradable

Uses: Signs, Box construction, Display stands



Translucent Polypropylene Sheets:

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Flexible
  • Can be folded and creased
  • Water resistant
  • Good for printing
  • Not fully transparent
  • Prone to scratches

Uses: Packaging, Stationery, Folders



Styrofoam:

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Lightweight
  • Easy to cut and shape
  • Good thermal insulation
  • Brittle and can snap easily
  • Not environmentally friendly

Uses: Modelling, Insulation, Formers for vacuum forming



Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE) Sheet:

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Flexible
  • Waterproof
  • Can be heat-sealed
  • Good chemical resistance
  • Not very strong
  • Not biodegradable

Uses: Wrapping, Packaging, Plastic bags



Plastazote Foam:

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Impact-resistant
  • Lightweight
  • Water-resistant
  • Good cushioning properties
  • Not biodegradable
  • Can be expensive

Uses: Protective packaging, Sports equipment padding, Orthotics



Cellulose Acetate:

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Transparent
  • Biodegradable
  • Good flexibility
  • Can be heat-sealed
  • Not very strong
  • Prone to tearing

Uses: Packaging, Film for photography, Overlays and laminates



Polylactide (PLA) Sheet and Film:

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Biodegradable
  • Can be composted
  • Good transparency
  • Good strength
  • Not heat-resistant
  • More expensive than traditional plastics

Uses: Biodegradable packaging, Food containers, Disposable cutlery
