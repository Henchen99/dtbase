Design communication

Communication and Presentation Techniques:

  • Used to convey proposals and intentions to clients, users, and manufacturers
  • Helps ensure clarity and precision in design communication
  • Includes both written and visual methods




Report Writing:

Purpose Examples
  • Presents detailed project findings
  • Documents design decisions and justifications
  • Used for formal communication with stakeholders
  • Project proposals
  • Design evaluations
  • Manufacturing documentation




Use of Graphs:

Purpose Examples
  • Visually represents data trends and comparisons
  • Helps illustrate performance metrics
  • Aids in decision-making and analysis
  • Material strength comparison
  • Product cost analysis
  • Energy efficiency trends




Tables and Charts:

Purpose Examples
  • Organizes complex information clearly
  • Allows for quick data comparisons
  • Used in technical specifications
  • Material properties comparison
  • Manufacturing process selection
  • Design cost breakdown




2D/3D Sketching:

Purpose Examples
  • Quickly conveys design ideas
  • Allows for initial concept visualization
  • Used in early-stage design development
  • Product concept sketches
  • Technical design drafts
  • Manufacturing layout plans




Mixed Media and Rendering:

Purpose Examples
  • Enhances visual appeal of sketches
  • Creates realistic product presentations
  • Used to showcase final concepts to clients
  • Digital renderings
  • Hand-drawn concept art
  • Marketing visuals




Dimensioning and Details for Manufacture:

Purpose Examples
  • Ensures precise measurements
  • Provides necessary information for production
  • Used in technical drawings and CAD files
  • Blueprints
  • Manufacturing guides
  • Engineering schematics
