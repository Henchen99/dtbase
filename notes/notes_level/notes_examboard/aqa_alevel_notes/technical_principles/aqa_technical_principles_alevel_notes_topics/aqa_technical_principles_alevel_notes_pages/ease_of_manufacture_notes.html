Design for Efficient Manufacture

Design Feature Purpose
Ribs and Webbing Used to reduce material thicknesses while maintaining strength and stability in the design.
Snap Fittings Eliminates the need for additional fixings or adhesives, reducing complexity and cost.
Internal Moulded Screw Posts Used with self-tapping screws, removing the need for separate fasteners and making assembly simpler.
Use of Pre-Made Components Leverages existing parts to reduce manufacturing time and cost.
Standardised Patterns and Sizes Using standardised sizes and patterns for easier and quicker assembly, aiding mass production.

Self Finishing Designs that require no further finishing processes once manufactured, reducing labor and cost.


Ribs and Webbing to Reduce Material Thicknesses:

  • Ribs and webbing help reinforce a product while reducing the overall material thickness.
  • This method allows for lighter products and cost-effective manufacturing by using less material.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Reduces material usage
  • Strengthens products with minimal weight
  • May require more complex molding processes
  • Increased material waste during manufacturing


Snap Fittings to Remove the Need for Fixings/Adhesives:

  • Snap fittings eliminate the need for screws or adhesives by allowing components to click together.
  • This method is quick to assemble, reducing time and labor costs in manufacturing.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Reduces the need for adhesives and screws
  • Quick and easy assembly process
  • May not provide as strong a connection as screws or adhesives
  • Limited to specific material types


Internal Moulded Screw Posts for Use with Self-Tapping Screws:

  • Internal moulded screw posts eliminate the need for external fasteners and improve product durability.
  • They provide a secure connection point for self-tapping screws.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Reduces the need for external fixings
  • Provides a secure and durable connection point
  • May complicate the moulding process
  • Requires accurate placement of screw posts


Use of Pre-Made Components:

  • Using pre-made components saves time in manufacturing and ensures consistent quality.
  • It reduces the need for custom tooling or complex processes.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Saves time and money in manufacturing
  • Ensures high consistency and quality
  • Less flexibility in design
  • May require inventory management


Use of Standardised Patterns and Sizes:

  • Standardized parts allow for more efficient manufacturing and easier sourcing of components.
  • They help reduce production costs and simplify assembly processes.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Cost-effective and time-efficient
  • Simplifies assembly and sourcing of components
  • Limits design flexibility
  • May not fit all products perfectly


Addition of Texture in Moulding to Reduce Number of Manufacturing Processes:

  • Adding texture to the mould surface can reduce the need for additional finishing processes, such as painting or coating.
  • This method can save time and reduce manufacturing costs.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Reduces the need for additional finishing steps
  • Cost-effective and time-saving
  • Texturing may limit design flexibility
  • Texture may wear over time with use


Self Finishing:

  • Self-finishing eliminates the need for additional post-processing such as painting or coating.
  • This can reduce production costs and shorten manufacturing time.
Advantages Disadvantages
  • Reduces production costs and time
  • Less post-processing required
  • Limited design options for finishing
  • May not always provide desired aesthetics
