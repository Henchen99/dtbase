Paper and board finishing

Laminating:

How it works:

  • Coating the paper with liquid/film to protect it

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Aesthetic/high quality look
  • Rigid/strengthens paper
  • Low cost
  • Hard to tear/rip
  • Paper hard to access/write on

Uses: Posters, pictures, song music

Embossing:

How it works:

    1. The paper is placed between the two formers
    2. The formers then close around the paper to form the shape
    3. Formers can be heated/cold

Advantages Disadvantages
  • High quality finish
  • Sophisticated appearance
  • Expensive

Uses: Toblerone card packaging, birthday cards

Debossing: The same process but recessed surface

Varnishing:

How it works:

  • Clear, non-pigmented ink used on pre-coated papers

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Enhances the colour of paper
  • Protects against dirt/fingerprints
  • Long dry time
  • Slows down overall completion time - slower production rate
  • Sticks to other surfaces/pages if not fully dried

Uses: Brochures, magazines

UV Varnishing:

How it works:

  • UV varnishing involves applying a clear varnish coating to paper or print products.
  • The varnish is cured under ultraviolet (UV) light to create a glossy, protective finish.
  • It can be applied over full or spot areas of a print.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Provides a high-gloss finish
  • Quick drying time due to UV curing
  • Enhances the appearance of printed products
  • Can be expensive due to the equipment and energy needed
  • Requires specialized UV curing equipment
  • Can create a strong shine that may be unsuitable for some designs

Uses: Business cards, brochures, packaging, high-end marketing materials

Spot Varnishing:

How it works:

  • Spot varnishing is a process where varnish is applied only to specific areas of a printed piece.
  • This selective application enhances certain parts of the design, often adding contrast or emphasis.
  • It is usually done after the printing process but before the final drying and cutting stages.

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Highlights specific areas of a print, creating a visual effect
  • Can be used for added texture or contrast in designs
  • Quick drying time when using UV varnish
  • Can be expensive due to additional processes
  • Limited to certain design styles that require specific areas to be highlighted
  • Requires precision to avoid overlapping the varnish

Uses: Luxury packaging, high-end marketing materials, business cards, brochures

Hot foil blocking:

How it works:

    1. A foil sheet is place between the paper and the die
    2. The die is then pressed onto the foil and heated to allow the foil to join to the paper
    3. The die is then removed and the foil cools

Advantages Disadvantages
  • Enhances/adds value to paper
  • Improved aesthetics
  • Range of colours/foils etc
  • If heated for too long the foil can bubble and melt
  • If heated not long enough the foil will not stick
  • Difficulty in reproducing tints and halftones

Uses: Passports, gold foil decorations, wedding invite cards
